Startup Panel Speakers

Joe Shim (Desktop Metal)
Joe graduated UIUC with a bachelors in Electrical Engineering Dec 2013. After graduation he moved to the Boston area to work at Analog Devices Inc as an applications engineer. During my time at Analog I also started a 3D Printing Company called Embark Industries as well as a Consulting Company named Quantom Engineering Consulting.

Currently, after some interesting turn of events, I am working at a startup named Desktop Metal doing research and development on a new type of 3D Printer that has the capability of printing metal without costing a fortune.
Will Steelman (Akuna Capital)
Will Steelman received his PhD in computer engineering in 2013 from Washinon University in St. Louis and currently serves as the Head of Execution Technology for Akuna Capital. He has a passion for high performance and heterogeneous computing and has academic and industry research experience using parallel software, FPGAs, GPUs, and embedded processors to solve computing problems.

Upon receiving his Bachelor's degree in 2005, Will worked in the medical imaging industry, focusing on R&D for 3D ultrasound probes. His work went on to be part of a commercialized ultrasound system that uses 3D ultrasound images to generate correlated biopsy and treatment plans for prostate cancer patients. In 2010, Will took his experience in processing real-time image data and applied it, in the finance industry, to processing real-time market data. There he worked for market data vendor Exegy, Inc, leading the systems engineering group. Exegy was one of the first vendors to apply heterogeneous computing, including FPGA technology, to the real-time processing of financial data. Will's experience with complex system architecture and market data led him to Akuna, where he was able to broaden his focus to include the entire trading pipeline from market data, to strategy development, to order generation. Currently Will leads the Execution Technology group at Akuna Capital in Chicago, where his group is responsible for researching and developing high performance trading engines used in Akuna's options market making business.

Akshay Singh (Quicket Solutions)
Akshay Singh, CTO & Board Member, Quicket Solutions, graduated in Computer Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2015. He has previously worked as an intern and co-ops in the product development at Caterpillar, Raytheon and Intel Corporation. Akshay's areas of technical expertise include database structures, driver development, data mining, and machine learning.
Akshay joined the company in early 2014 and immediately began demonstrating his exceptional technical expertise and leadership qualities. He single-handedly designed the cloud architecture of Quicket and lead various engineering teams to develop numerous core features for Quicket's system. He currently leads Quicket's entire Engineering and Research & Development. He is also involved in company's sales and strategy operations. Akshay's passion for creating next generation technology solutions from law enforcement and civilian government agencies will continue to be a cornerstone of Quicket's current product development and R&D.
